U.S. Digital Service

Tour of Duty: 3 months – 4 years.

The U.S. Digital Service is a group of geeks working across the Federal Government that uses design and technology to deliver better services to the American people. USDS was created in 2014 to transform critical, public-facing digital services by applying modern technology best practices. To accomplish our mission, we hire top technologists into government for short-term tours of duty.

USDS is seeking software engineers, technical product managers, and designers for tours of duty of 3 months to 4 years.

For more information about a tour of duty, click here.

Technology Transformation Services

TTS, as part of the General Services Administration, is improving government efficiency and providing better service to the public by helping agencies build, buy, and share technology. There are several programs under TTS, including 18F and the Presidential Innovation Fellows (PIF) program.

TTS/18F

Tour of Duty: 12 months – 4 years.

18F develops partnerships with agencies to help them build or buy digital services that instill pride and trust in government services, that reliably meet user needs, that implement strong security practices, and that are delivered on time and in a cost effective manner. Through our work together, we also aim to generate improvements in agency practices that outlast our formal partnerships.

18F is a remote-first team with offices in D.C., New York, Chicago, and San Francisco, and has teammates working all over the country. Staff serve between 1 and 4 years. For more information about a tour of duty with 18F, email join18f@gsa.gov.

Presidential Innovation Fellows (PIF)

Tour of Duty: 12 months – 18 months.

The Presidential Innovation Fellows (PIF) program is looking for mid- to senior-career individuals with a proven track record of success in areas such as entrepreneurship, strategic design, technology, innovation and modern product management.

Fellows team up with agency changemakers to tackle the nation’s biggest and most pressing challenges, such as improving access to education, fueling job creation and the economy, and expanding the public’s ability to access their personal health data.

Fellows serve 12-month tours of duty in Washington, D.C. Classes typically start in late summer or early fall. For more information about a tour of duty with PIF, email pif-application@gsa.gov.

The “tour of duty model” allows for leaders in the tech industry to pair with dedicated civil servants for a full time engagement of anywhere from three months to four years.

Three federal programs, the United States Digital Service, 18F, and the Presidential Innovation Fellows program bring top industry talent to critical areas of innovation within the government.
